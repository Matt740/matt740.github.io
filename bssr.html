Blue Sky Solar Racing

Blue Sky Solar Racing is University of Toronto's solar racing team. I started working with Blue Sky Solar Racing in my first year at the University of Toronto. Since then I have had the opportunity to work on numerous interesting engineering projects on the Structural/Fabrication team, Mechanical team, and most recently as the System Integration Director of the team. Here are some of my favorite projects from my time on the team.

Front Lights

Design, manufacture, and testing of front daytime running lights and indicators according to regulations.

Hub and Axle Design

Detailed design of the hub and axle assembly.

Battery Box

Manufacture and fixture design of the structural and mechanical components.

Vacuum Infusion

Manufacture of top aerobody. Dry layups and vacuum infusion.

Tuft Testing

Detailed testing, design, and application of tufts for flow visualization of the car.

Array Installation

Manufacture of top solar array for solar car. Placement, wiring, and soldering of modules.

Car Shipment

Final preperations for the shipment of the solar car to Australia.

Sandwich Panel Layups

Wet layups and vacuum bagging to make various composite parts.

Testing

Assortment of static, low-speed, high-speed, and long-distance testing with the team.
